Supply, Demand, and the Business That Sells It

By Amana Capital East Africa – Investor Education Series

In Module 1, we explored the broad economy. Now we zoom in to the individual company. The most fundamental tool for understanding a business is the law of supply and demand — the same force that determines the price of onions in Kariakoo also determines the profits of TBL, VODA, and CRDB.

The Basics

Supply is how much producers are willing to sell at a given price. Higher prices usually mean more supply.

Demand is how much consumers are willing to buy. Lower prices boost demand.

Equilibrium is where supply meets demand — the market price.

For a business, the goal is to sell at a price well above the cost of production. The difference is profit.

Pricing PowerNot all companies can raise prices without losing customers. A company with pricing power can increase prices steadily over time without significantly reducing demand. This power comes from:

Strong brand (TBL’s Safari Lager commands loyalty).

Few competitors (Tanzania’s cement industry has limited players, aiding TCC and TPCC).

High switching costs (a bank where a client holds a mortgage, salary account, and mobile banking is hard to leave).

Companies with pricing power are generally better investments because they protect margins during inflation and can reinvest profits for growth.

Demand ShiftsDemand changes due to rising incomes, population growth, or new technology. As Tanzania’s middle class expands, demand for financial services, mobile data, and branded goods increases, benefiting CRDB, NMB, and VODA. When the government invests in infrastructure, demand for cement rises, boosting TCC and TPCC. In the Daily DSE Wrap, turnover and volume reveal where demand is flowing in real time.

SupplySide RisksEven strong demand won’t help a company that can’t deliver. Drought, power outages, or import delays can crush margins. For example, TOL, which imports fuel, faces supply risks if global oil prices spike or the shilling weakens. Ask: Where do raw materials come from? How dependent is the company on imports? Does it have reliable power and water?
