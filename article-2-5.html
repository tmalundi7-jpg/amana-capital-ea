ompetitive Moats: Why Some Companies Stay on Top

By Amana Capital East Africa � Investor Education Series

Why does one bank dominate lending year after year? Why can a brewery raise prices without losing customers? The answer is a competitive moat � a durable advantage that protects a company from rivals, just as a medieval moat protected a castle. Understanding moats is central to long-term investing success.

Types of Competitive Moats

Moat Type What It Means DSE Example

Brand Consumers trust and prefer the brand, paying a premium. TBL�s Safari Lager, Kilimanjaro Beer � strong local loyalty.

Cost advantage The company can produce at a lower cost than competitors, either through scale, superior technology, or access to cheap raw materials. TPCC�s local limestone reserves give it a cost edge in cement.

Network effect The service becomes more valuable as more people use it. VODA�s mobile money network: every new user makes the platform more useful for all.

Regulatory licence The government limits competition through licensing. Banks need a banking licence from the BOT, creating a barrier to entry.

High switching costs It is expensive or inconvenient for customers to leave. A corporate client with multiple loan facilities, payroll services, and a relationship manager at CRDB is unlikely to switch to another bank easily.

How to Identify a Moat

Ask these questions:

Can the company raise prices without losing significant sales? If yes, it has pricing power � a strong sign of a moat.

Does it consistently earn higher returns on equity (ROE) than its peers? A moated company should be more profitable over time.

Has its market share been stable or growing? A wide moat helps defend market share.

Would a new competitor struggle to replicate what this company does? If the answer is yes, the moat is real.

Moats and the DSE: What to Watch

In the Daily DSE Wrap, we often comment on sector dynamics that hint at moat strength or weakness. When a company like NMB reports higher loan growth than its peers, it might be leveraging its branch network (a moat). When TBL�s volumes hold up despite a price increase, brand loyalty is at work.

A moat is not permanent. New technology (mobile banking disrupting physical branches), regulatory changes, or poor management can erode a moat. That�s why we track industry trends closely.

The Daily DSE Wrap Connection

When we discuss a company�s competitive position in the Daily Wrap, we are evaluating its moat. For example, if a new entrant starts taking market share from an established player, we would flag it as a potential moat erosion. Conversely, if a company announces a major capital investment that will widen its cost advantage, we would note the strengthening moat.

Action step: Pick your favourite DSE stock. Write down what you believe its competitive moat is. Then test that belief: could a competitor with enough money replicate it? If the answer is yes, the moat may be narrower than you think.
