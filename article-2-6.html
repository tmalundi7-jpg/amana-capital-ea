Article 2.6 - How Management Decisions Create (or Destroy) Value

By Amana Capital East Africa - Investor Education Series

Even a great company with a wide moat can be ruined by poor management. Conversely, a mediocre business can be transformed by an exceptional leadership team.

Five Key Management Decisions

Capital allocation: Invests in high-return projects; returns excess cash to shareholders. Warning: chasing growth for its own sake, expensive acquisitions.

Capital structure: Maintains prudent debt; matches funding to asset life. Warning: excessive short-term debt.

Dividend policy: Sustainable, growing dividends, retaining enough for reinvestment. Warning: sudden cuts without explanation; borrowing to pay dividends.

Transparency: Open about strategy, risks, performance. Warning: complex language, frequent accounting changes.

Culture & Governance: Ethical, long-oriented; independent board. Warning: conflicts of interest, family members in key roles without merit.

Assessing Management on the DSE

Read the chairman’s and CEO’s letters. Are they honest about challenges?

Compare ROE over time. Consistent ROE suggests disciplined capital allocation.

Check insider trading announcements. Heavy director selling without reason is a red flag.

Review dividend history. Stable or growing dividends signal confidence.

In the Daily Wrap, we sometimes note management announcements - a major investment, a CEO change - and analyse through this lens.
