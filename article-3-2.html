Article 3.2 - Stocks, Bonds, and ETFs: The Three Building Blocks

By Amana Capital East Africa - Investor Education Series

On the DSE, you buy specific securities, each with its own risk, return, and purpose.

Stocks (Equities) Buying a share of CRDB or TBL makes you a part-owner. You share in profits (dividends, capital gains) and risks. Stocks offer the highest long-term return potential but are the most volatile.

Bonds A bond is a loan to the government or a company in exchange for fixed interest (coupon) and return of principal at maturity. Bonds are generally safer, offering lower returns. They suit conservative investors or as a portfolio stabiliser. The DSE bond market is active; on 29 June 2026, TZS 16.20 billion in bonds traded, with a 25-year bond yielding 15.95% heavily traded.

ETFs (Exchange-Traded Funds) An ETF holds a basket of stocks or bonds and trades like a single stock. The DSE lists IEACLC-ETF and VERTEX-ETF. ETFs provide instant diversification. On 29 June, IEACLC-ETF traded 354,823 shares at TZS 1,250.

Portfolio Building A balanced investor might hold 60% equities, 30% bonds, 10% cash. The exact mix depends on age, goals, and risk tolerance.
