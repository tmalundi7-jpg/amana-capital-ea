Article 3.4 - Understanding Market Data: Turnover, Volume, and Deals

By Amana Capital East Africa - Investor Education Series

Every trading day, the DSE publishes numbers like “Turnover: TZS 7,088.98 million, Volume: 1,734,477 shares, Deals: 4,704.” They are the market’s voice.

Turnover: The Money Flow Total value of shares traded. A rising turnover trend suggests growing interest and confidence. A sudden spike without a price move may indicate a large institutional block trade. On 29 June 2026, turnover was TZS 7.09 billion - down from a record but still well above the quiet-day level of TZS 3 billion, signalling a liquid, active market.

Volume: The Quantity of Shares Traded High volume on CRDB (928,067 shares) indicates deep liquidity - you can trade a large block without dramatically moving the price. A rising price on expanding volume confirms genuine demand. A rising price on declining volume may be fragile.

Deals: The Participation Count Separate buy-sell transactions. Many deals with moderate volume = broad retail participation. Few deals with high volume = large block trades. On 29 June, 4,704 deals with moderate volume indicated healthy, domestically driven activity.

Putting It Together Strong, broad-based buying: turnover surges, volume jumps, deals spike, DSEI rises. A single large cross-trade: turnover rises but volume and deals fall, DSEI barely moves.
