Article 3.5 - The Difference Between Primary and Secondary Markets

By Amana Capital East Africa - Investor Education Series

When you buy CRDB shares through your broker on a Tuesday, you’re in the secondary market - trading already-issued securities. But CRDB originally sold shares to the public in an IPO in the primary market.

Primary Market: Companies Raise Fresh Capital The issuer sells new securities directly to investors. Proceeds go to the company. Examples:

IPO (Initial Public Offering): A company lists on the DSE for the first time.

Rights issue: Existing shareholders buy additional shares at a discount.

Bond auction: Government or corporation issues new bonds.

Primary offerings affect existing shareholders (dilution in a rights issue) and signal growth plans. The Daily Wrap covers these when announced.

Secondary Market: Investors Trade Among Themselves This is the DSE’s daily trading - buying and selling between investors. No new capital flows to the company. It provides liquidity and fair price discovery. The DSEI reflects secondary market prices.

Why the Distinction Matters

IPO shares are often priced at a discount; secondary trading determines the real price.

A rights issue dilutes your ownership unless you participate.

Buying a bond at auction and holding to maturity avoids price fluctuations; buying in the secondary market exposes you to interest rate risk.
