Article 3.7 - Understanding and Using ETFs on the DSE

By Amana Capital East Africa - Investor Education Series

Exchange-traded funds, or ETFs, are one of the simplest ways to build a diversified portfolio. Two ETFs are listed on the Dar es Salaam Stock Exchange, and they trade just like ordinary shares. If you have ever felt overwhelmed by the choice of individual stocks, an ETF might be the right tool for you.

What is an ETF? An ETF is a basket of securities - stocks, bonds, or both - that is packaged into a single tradable fund. When you buy one share of an ETF, you are effectively buying a tiny slice of every company or bond held inside it. The price of the ETF moves up and down as the value of the underlying assets changes.

The two DSE ETFs

IEACLC-ETF (iTrust ETF): This fund invests primarily in East African equities. It gives you exposure to multiple countries and sectors in a single trade.

VERTEX-ETF: A smaller, more recently established fund that also holds a diversified portfolio of East African assets.

Both ETFs trade on the DSE, and you can buy or sell them through any licensed broker, just as you would with CRDB or NMB shares.

Why investors use ETFs

Instant diversification: With one purchase, you own a piece of dozens of companies. This reduces the risk that any single company’s problems will hurt your portfolio.

Simplicity: You do not need to research individual stocks, read annual reports, or worry about sector weights. The fund manager does that for you.

Low cost: ETFs typically have lower fees than actively managed unit trusts, because they simply track an index rather than paying a team of analysts to pick stocks.

How to buy an ETF The process is exactly the same as buying a stock. You contact your broker, specify the ETF (e.g., IEACLC-ETF), the number of shares you want, and your price limit. Your broker places the order on the DSE, and the shares are settled into your CSD account within two business days.

What to watch for ETFs are generally liquid, but on some days trading volumes can be low. Before placing an order, check the current bid and ask prices on the DSE daily price table, and use a limit order - never a market order - to avoid paying more than you intended. Also note that ETF prices can differ slightly from the net asset value (NAV) of the underlying securities. A small premium or discount is normal; a persistent gap of more than 3% may signal an opportunity or a risk.

ETFs and your portfolio For a beginner, an ETF can be the core of your portfolio. For a more experienced investor, an ETF can serve as a foundation, with individual stocks added around it to express specific views. In every issue of the Daily DSE Wrap, we report the day’s ETF trading activity so you can stay informed.

Put It Into Practice Look up the latest DSE daily price table on our website. Find the IEACLC-ETF and VERTEX-ETF. Note the closing price, volume, and the spread between the best bid and best ask. If you were to invest TZS 500,000, how many shares could you buy at the current ask price?
