Article 4.1 - What Is Fundamental Analysis? The Art of Finding True Value

By Amana Capital East Africa - Investor Education Series

You now understand the economy, companies, and the marketplace. Fundamental analysis is the process of evaluating a company’s intrinsic value by examining its financial statements, competitive position, industry, and the broader economy. If intrinsic value is significantly above the market price, the stock may be undervalued - a buying opportunity.

Price vs. Value Benjamin Graham taught that in the short run the market is a voting machine (popularity), but in the long run it is a weighing machine (substance). Your job is to estimate substance - the true worth - and compare it to the current market price. Intrinsic value is a range, based on reasonable assumptions about future cash flows, growth, and risk.

Two Main Approaches

Relative valuation: Compare the company’s price to earnings, book value, or sales, then compare those multiples to peers or history (covered in 4.2).

Discounted cash flow (DCF): Project future free cash flows and discount them back to today’s shillings. More complex but forces deep thinking.

For most DSE investors, relative valuation using simple multiples is a practical start.

The Fundamental Analysis Checklist

Understand the business.

Assess the industry.

Examine financials (revenue, margins, debt, cash flow).

Evaluate management.

Calculate valuation.

Consider macro risks (interest rates, exchange rates, regulation).
