Article 4.3 - How to Analyse a Sector: Banking

By Amana Capital East Africa - Investor Education Series

Banks dominate the DSE. CRDB, NMB, KCB, DCB, and others are among the most traded. Analysing a bank is different because its raw material is money.

The Banking Business Model Banks take deposits (pay interest) and lend (charge higher interest). The difference is the net interest margin (NIM). They also earn fee income from transactions, forex, and mobile banking.

Key drivers: loan book growth, fee income, cost control. Key risks: credit risk (defaults), interest rate risk, liquidity risk.

Metrics That Matter

NIM: 5-8% is typical in Tanzania.

Return on Equity (ROE): Above 15% is good.

Return on Assets (ROA): Above 1.5% is good.

Cost-to-Income Ratio: Below 50% is efficient.

NPL Ratio: Below 5% is comfortable.

Loan-to-Deposit Ratio: 70-90% balances profitability and liquidity.

Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR): Above the regulatory minimum (12%+).

Step-by-Step Bank Analysis

Read the annual report.

Calculate NIM, ROE, ROA, cost-to-income for three years.

Examine NPL ratio. Which sectors are struggling?

Check CAR.

Assess competitive position.

Consider macro environment.
