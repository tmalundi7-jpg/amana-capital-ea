ow to Analyse a Sector: Banking

By Amana Capital East Africa – Investor Education Series

Banks are the backbone of the DSE, accounting for a significant portion of total market capitalisation and daily turnover. CRDB, NMB, KCB, DCB, MKCB, MCB, and MUCOBA are among the most actively traded counters. Analysing a bank is different from analysing a manufacturer because a bank’s raw material is money itself. This article gives you a specialised toolkit.

The Banking Business Model

A bank takes deposits (pays interest to savers) and lends that money to borrowers (charges a higher rate). The difference — the net interest margin (NIM) — is its core profit. Banks also earn fee income from transaction services, foreign exchange, and mobile banking.

Key revenue drivers:

Loan book growth: More lending, more interest income.

Fee income: Non‑interest revenue from services.

Cost control: Efficient operations (low cost‑to‑income ratio).

Key risks:

Credit risk: Borrowers default. Watch the non‑performing loan (NPL) ratio.

Interest rate risk: If deposit rates rise faster than lending rates, margins compress.

Liquidity risk: A sudden withdrawal of deposits can strain the bank.

The Metrics That Matter

Metric What It Tells You Healthy Range

Net Interest Margin (NIM) The spread between lending and deposit rates. 5–8% is typical for Tanzanian banks

Return on Equity (ROE) How profitably the bank uses shareholders’ capital. Above 15% is good; above 20% is excellent

Return on Assets (ROA) Profit generated per shilling of assets. Above 1.5% is good

Cost‑to‑Income Ratio How much it costs to generate a shilling of revenue. Below 50% is efficient

NPL Ratio Percentage of loans that are not being repaid. Below 5% is comfortable

Loan‑to‑Deposit Ratio How much of the deposit base is lent out. 70–90% balances profitability and liquidity

Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR) The bank’s cushion against losses, as required by BOT. Above the regulatory minimum (12%+)

When we discuss a banking stock in the Daily DSE Wrap, we often reference these metrics. For example, if NMB reports a strong quarter, we might highlight its ROE or its improving NPL ratio.

How to Analyse a Bank Step‑by‑Step

Read the annual report, focusing on the management discussion of the loan book, deposits, and strategy.

Calculate NIM, ROE, ROA, and the cost‑to‑income ratio for the last three years. Look for trends.

Examine the NPL ratio. Is it rising? If so, which sectors (agriculture, SMEs, personal) are struggling?

Check the capital adequacy ratio. A bank with a CAR well above the minimum has more room to grow its loan book.

Assess the competitive position. Does the bank have a wide branch network? A strong mobile platform? Loyal corporate clients?

Consider the macro environment. Are interest rates rising or falling? Is the economy growing? These directly affect bank profitability.

The Daily DSE Wrap Connection

The Banking, Finance & Investment (BI) index is reported in every Daily Wrap. When you see it rise, it often reflects positive sentiment about loan growth and economic expansion. When you see a surge in a specific bank’s volume — like NMB’s block trade on 29 June — it signals institutional positioning, which you can now analyse using the metrics above.

Action step: Pick one DSE bank. Download its most recent annual report. Calculate its NIM, ROE, and NPL ratio. Compare to another DSE bank. Which is stronger?
