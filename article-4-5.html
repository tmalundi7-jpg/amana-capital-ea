Article 4.5 - Reading the Story Behind the Numbers: Annual Report Analysis

By Amana Capital East Africa - Investor Education Series

The annual report is the single most important document a public company produces. It contains the financial statements, management’s narrative, the auditor’s opinion, and detailed notes.

Where to Start

Chairman’s / CEO’s letter: Sets the tone. Does management acknowledge challenges? Look for specific goals and whether previous goals were met.

Auditor’s report: Look for “unqualified opinion.” Any “qualified” opinion is a red flag.

Financial statements: Income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement.

Notes to the financial statements: Often the most revealing part. They explain accounting policies, breakdown debt, detail contingent liabilities, and disclose related-party transactions.

Red Flags

Frequent changes in accounting policies.

Large related-party transactions not at arm’s length.

Rising receivables outpacing revenue growth.

Overly complex language.

Dividend cut without explanation.
