Article 4.6 - An Introduction to Technical Analysis (It’s Not Sorcery)

By Amana Capital East Africa - Investor Education Series

While fundamental analysis tells you what to buy, technical analysis attempts to tell you when by studying price charts, volume, and patterns. It’s a supplementary tool, not a crystal ball.

The Foundation: Price and Trends A trend is the general direction of prices. An uptrend has higher highs and higher lows; a downtrend has lower lows and lower highs. The simplest rule: Don’t fight the trend. A rising price with rising volume suggests accumulation; a falling price with high volume suggests distribution.

Support and Resistance

Support: A price level where buying interest prevents further decline (a floor).

Resistance: A price level where selling pressure halts a rally (a ceiling).

If CRDB consistently finds buyers at TZS 2,550, that’s support; if it fails to break above TZS 2,600, that’s resistance.

Volume as Confirmation A breakout above resistance on significantly higher volume is more likely genuine. A breakout on low volume may be false.

Simple Moving Averages (SMA) A 50-day SMA is the average closing price over the last 50 trading days, smoothing noise. Price above the 50-day SMA suggests a positive medium-term trend; below, negative.

Limitations on the DSE Technical analysis works best in liquid, continuous markets. The DSE’s lower liquidity on some counters means a few large orders can create misleading patterns. Use it as a supplement to, never a replacement for, fundamental analysis.
