Article 5.1 - Risk and Return: The Only Free Lunch Is Diversification

By Amana Capital East Africa - Investor Education Series

Every investment carries risk. The key is not to avoid it - impossible - but to understand, manage, and be compensated for bearing it.

Types of Investment Risk

Company-specific risk: Can be diversified away by holding many companies.

Sector risk: Partially diversifiable by holding different sectors.

Market risk (systematic): Affects all assets. Managed through asset allocation and a long time horizon.

The Risk-Return Trade-Off Higher expected return demands higher risk. Government bonds are safe with lower returns; equities offer higher returns but can lose significant value.

Diversification: The Free Lunch Spreading money across different companies, sectors, and asset classes reduces risk without necessarily reducing expected return. A simple diversified DSE portfolio might hold a major bank (CRDB/NMB), a consumer goods company (TBL), an industrial (TCC/TPCC), a telecom (VODA), and a bond or bond fund.
