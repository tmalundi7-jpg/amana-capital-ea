Article 5.5 - Long-Horizon Thinking: The Amana Capital Philosophy

By Amana Capital East Africa - Investor Education Series

At Amana Capital East Africa, our investment philosophy rests on four pillars: Integrity without exception, Long-horizon thinking, Deep regional intelligence, and Disciplined conviction.

Integrity Without Exception Trust is the foundation of finance. We are transparent, clearly separate education from advice, and never promise returns we cannot deliver. For you: invest only in what you understand. If a product sounds too good to be true, walk away.

Long-Horizon Thinking The best investment decisions are made with a multi-year perspective. Short-term price movements are noise; long-term value creation matters. Our daily analysis always includes a “Considerations for a Multi-Year Framework” to train your eye beyond daily volatility. For you: set a minimum holding period of, say, three years. Ask: Will this company likely be stronger in three years?

Deep Regional Intelligence We focus on East Africa because that’s what we know best - the local regulatory environment, cultural nuances, and economic drivers. For you: build your portfolio primarily in markets you understand. A Tanzanian investor holding only DSE stocks and bonds is perfectly fine.

Disciplined Conviction Analysis without action is useless. Action without discipline is dangerous. We form a view, act within a structured framework, and hold ourselves accountable. For you: create an Investment Policy Statement - one page stating your goals, allocation, and buying/selling rules. When emotions run high, your policy keeps you grounded.

Action Step: Write your own Investment Policy Statement. “I will invest TZS 200,000 per month into a diversified portfolio of DSE equities and a bond fund, and I will not sell for at least five years.” Sign it. Keep it.
