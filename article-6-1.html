How to Open a CSD Account and Buy Your First DSE Stock

By Amana Capital East Africa – Investor Education Series

All the knowledge in the world is useless without action. Here’s exactly how to start.

Open a CSD Account

Select a licensed stockbroker from the DSE’s official list (available on the DSE website or through our contact page).

Visit the broker’s office with:

Copy of your TIN (Taxpayer Identification Number).

Passportsized photograph.

Copy of national ID, passport, or driver’s licence.

Proof of residence (utility bill or bank statement).

Fill out the CSD account opening forms.

The broker submits your application; you receive an account number.

Fund your brokerage account (minimums can be as low as TZS 100,000).

Place Your First TradeContact your broker and specify:

Ticker (e.g., CRDB)

Quantity (e.g., 100 shares)

Price limit (e.g., “buy at TZS 2,550 maximum”)The broker executes the order on the DSE ATS. Shares are credited to your CSD account (T+2). You receive a contract note.

Tips for Beginners

Start small with a liquid, wellknown company you’ve researched.

Use limit orders, not market orders, to control the price.

Hold for the long term; don’t check prices daily hoping to flip quick profits.

Further Reading: For those living abroad, see Investing from Abroad: A Diaspora StepbyStep Guide.

Action Step: If you haven’t already, call or visit a licensed broker this week and start the CSD account process.
