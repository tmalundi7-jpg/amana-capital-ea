nvesting from Abroad: A Diaspora Step‑by‑Step Guide

By Amana Capital East Africa – Investor Education Series

Tanzanians living in the UK, Europe, North America, or Asia can invest on the DSE. Distance is not a barrier, but the process requires some additional steps. This guide covers the essentials.

Opening a CSD Account from Abroad

Many licensed DSE brokers can handle the account opening process remotely. You will typically need:

Scanned copies of your Tanzanian passport or national ID.

A TIN certificate (you can apply through the Tanzania Revenue Authority’s online portal or via a representative).

Proof of overseas address (utility bill, bank statement).

Passport‑sized photograph.

Completed account opening forms (provided by the broker).

Contact a broker directly, explain you are in the diaspora, and ask for their remote account opening procedure. Some brokers may require original documents couriered, but many now accept email scans initially.

Funding Your Account

You will need to transfer funds in hard currency (GBP, USD, EUR) to the broker’s settlement bank account. The funds will be converted to TZS at the prevailing exchange rate. Compare exchange rates and transfer fees — specialist services (Wise, WorldRemit, direct SWIFT) may offer better rates than traditional banks.

Once your shillings are in the brokerage account, you can place orders as any local investor would.

Tax Considerations

Withholding tax on dividends: typically 5% for resident individuals. For non‑resident individuals, rates may differ under double taxation agreements. Consult a tax advisor in your country of residence.

Capital gains tax: Currently, gains on listed securities are tax‑exempt in Tanzania, but your country of residence may tax them. Check local rules.

Estate tax: Ensure your CSD account and holdings are documented in your will or estate plan, as cross‑border probate can be complex.

The Daily DSE Wrap Connection

As a diaspora investor, you can read the Daily Wrap at any time from anywhere. It keeps you connected to the home market and alerts you to opportunities. The exchange rate data we provide daily helps you calculate exactly how many shillings your next remittance will buy.

Action step: Email three DSE brokers this week, stating you are a diaspora investor and asking about their remote account opening process. Choose the one that responds most clearly and professionally.
