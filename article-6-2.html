Article 6.2 - Investing from Abroad: A Diaspora Step-by-Step Guide

By Amana Capital East Africa - Investor Education Series

Tanzanians living in the UK, Europe, North America, or Asia can invest on the DSE. Distance is not a barrier, but there are additional steps.

Opening a CSD Account from Abroad Many licensed DSE brokers handle remote account opening. You’ll typically need:

Scanned copies of your Tanzanian passport or national ID.

A TIN certificate (apply through the TRA online portal or via a representative).

Proof of overseas address (utility bill, bank statement).

Passport-sized photograph.

Completed account opening forms (provided by the broker).

Contact a broker directly, explain you are in the diaspora, and ask for their remote account opening procedure.

Funding Your Account Transfer funds in hard currency (GBP, USD, EUR) to the broker’s settlement bank account. Funds are converted to TZS at the prevailing exchange rate. Compare exchange rates and fees - specialist services (Wise, WorldRemit, direct SWIFT) may offer better rates than traditional banks. Once shillings are in your brokerage account, you place orders as any local investor would.

Tax Considerations

Withholding tax on dividends: typically 5% for resident individuals. For non-residents, rates may differ under double taxation agreements. Consult a tax advisor.

Capital gains tax: Currently, gains on listed securities are tax-exempt in Tanzania. Your country of residence may tax them. Check local rules.

Estate tax: Ensure your CSD account and holdings are documented in your will or estate plan.
