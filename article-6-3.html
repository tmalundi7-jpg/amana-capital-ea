nderstanding Tanzanian Tax on Investments

By Amana Capital East Africa � Investor Education Series

Taxes can significantly affect your net investment return. Fortunately, Tanzania�s tax regime for listed securities is relatively favourable compared to many countries. This article outlines the main taxes you will encounter as a DSE investor.

Withholding Tax on Dividends

Dividends paid by DSE-listed companies are subject to a 5% final withholding tax for resident individuals. This means that if a company declares a dividend of TZS 1,000 per share, TZS 950 is deposited into your brokerage account, and the remaining TZS 50 goes to the TRA. The tax is �final,� meaning you do not need to declare it further on your personal income tax return.

For non-resident individuals, the rate may vary based on the double taxation agreement between Tanzania and your country of residence. Common rates range from 5% to 15%.

Capital Gains Tax on Listed Securities

As of the current tax code, capital gains from the sale of listed securities are exempt from tax for both residents and non-residents. This is a major advantage of investing through the DSE. A gain of TZS 1,000,000 is yours entirely.

This exemption is designed to encourage long-term investment and market participation. However, tax laws can change, so stay informed.

Withholding Tax on Bond Interest

Interest earned on government bonds and corporate bonds is subject to a 5% withholding tax for residents. For non-residents, the rate may be higher under certain conditions.

Tax on Collective Investment Schemes

If you invest through a unit trust like UTT AMIS, the tax treatment depends on the underlying assets. Generally, fund income distributed to you is subject to withholding tax at the applicable rate.

Record-Keeping

Keep all contract notes, dividend vouchers, and annual CSD statements. They are your proof of transactions and will be needed if you are ever audited or if you need to reconcile your portfolio.

The Daily DSE Wrap Connection

When we report dividend announcements in the Daily Wrap, we often state the gross dividend and the net (after-tax) amount. Knowing the tax rate allows you to calculate your true income. For example, the NMB dividend of TZS 610.15 gross yields TZS 579.64 net after the 5% tax.

Action step: If you hold dividend-paying stocks, check your contract notes to confirm that withholding tax has been correctly applied. If uncertain, contact your broker.
