Article 6.4 - Using Collective Investment Schemes (UTT AMIS, etc.)

By Amana Capital East Africa - Investor Education Series

Not everyone has the time or expertise to build a diversified portfolio of individual stocks. Collective investment schemes (CIS) - also called unit trusts or mutual funds - pool money from many investors and are managed by professionals. In Tanzania, UTT AMIS is one of the primary providers.

How a Unit Trust Works

You buy units at the prevailing unit price (Net Asset Value per unit, NAV).

The fund invests in a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or both.

Returns come from capital gains on underlying securities and interest/dividend income.

You can buy and redeem units on any dealing day.

Advantages

Instant diversification even with small amounts.

Professional management - the fund manager conducts analysis and trading.

Liquidity - you can usually redeem units relatively quickly.

Regulatory oversight - CIS are regulated by CMSA, providing a layer of protection.

Types of Funds

Equity funds: Invest primarily in stocks. Higher risk, higher potential return.

Bond funds: Invest in government and corporate bonds. Lower risk, stable income.

Balanced funds: Mix of equities and bonds.

Money market funds: Very low risk, highly liquid, often used as a cash management tool.

How to Invest

Visit the fund manager’s office or website.

Complete an application form and provide KYC documents (ID, TIN, photo).

Deposit the minimum investment amount (often TZS 100,000 or less).

Receive a statement showing your unit holdings.

Monitor the fund’s performance and add to your investment regularly.
