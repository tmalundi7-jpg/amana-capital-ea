Building a Monthly Investment Habit

By Amana Capital East Africa – Investor Education Series

The most successful investors are the most consistent. Building a monthly investment habit — automatically, regardless of market conditions — is the surest path to long‑term wealth.

Shilling‑Cost Averaging

Instead of investing a large lump sum at once, you invest a fixed amount at regular intervals (e.g., TZS 200,000 every month). When prices are high, your fixed amount buys fewer shares; when prices are low, it buys more. Over time, your average cost per share is lower than the average price, because you automatically bought more during dips. This removes the need to time the market and reduces emotional stress.

How to Set It Up

  • Determine your monthly investment amount (what you can comfortably set aside after essential expenses).
  • Choose one or two well‑diversified instruments: a balanced unit trust, or 2–3 blue‑chip stocks you intend to hold for years.
  • Set up a standing order from your bank account to your brokerage or fund account on a fixed date each month.
  • Instruct your broker to buy the specified shares or fund units as soon as the funds arrive.

Even TZS 100,000 per month will compound into substantial wealth over decades.

The Psychological Benefit

Monthly investing removes constant decision‑making. You are no longer asking, “Is now a good time to buy?” because the answer is always yes — you’re investing for 2035, not next week. The Daily DSE Wrap becomes a source of information and comfort, not a trigger for action.

APPLY IT NOW

If you haven’t already, set up a standing order this week for a monthly investment amount. Start small if necessary. The habit itself is more important than the amount.
