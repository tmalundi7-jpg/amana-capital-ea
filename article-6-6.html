Article 6.6 - A Practical Guide to Choosing a Stockbroker and Using the CSD

By Amana Capital East Africa - Investor Education Series

All the knowledge in the world is useless if you cannot execute a trade. To buy or sell shares on the Dar es Salaam Stock Exchange, you need a licensed stockbroker and a Central Securities Depository (CSD) account. This guide explains how to choose the right broker for your needs and how to use the CSD effectively - even if you are investing from abroad.

1. What is a stockbroker? A stockbroker is a firm licensed by the Capital Markets and Securities Authority (CMSA) to execute trades on the DSE on behalf of clients. Your broker is your gateway to the market. You place your orders through them, and they ensure your shares are settled correctly.

2. How to choose a broker There are several licensed brokers in Tanzania. When selecting one, consider these factors:

Reputation and track record: How long has the broker been operating? Are they known for reliability and good customer service?

Fees and commissions: Brokers charge a commission on each trade, typically a small percentage of the transaction value. Compare rates, but remember that the cheapest broker is not always the best if their service is slow or unresponsive.

Access to research: Some brokers provide their own market analysis, company reports, or trade recommendations. This can be a valuable addition to the independent research you do yourself.

Technology platform: Does the broker offer an online or mobile platform where you can view your holdings, place orders, and see market data? For busy investors, a good digital platform is essential.

Customer service: When you call or email, do they respond promptly? Can you reach your assigned dealer directly? Responsive service matters, especially when you need to act quickly.

3. The broker you choose is not permanent You can switch brokers if you are unsatisfied. The process involves transferring your CSD account, which your new broker can help facilitate.

4. The CSD account: your electronic safe The Central Securities Depository holds your shares in electronic form. You do not receive paper certificates. Your CSD account records exactly what you own - stocks, bonds, and ETFs. You need a CSD account before you can trade, and your broker will open one for you as part of the account-opening process.

5. Using the CSD account

Check your holdings: You can request a CSD statement at any time, either through your broker or directly from the DSE’s CSD portal. This shows all your holdings and recent transactions. Make it a habit to check your statement at least quarterly.

Dividends: When a company pays a dividend, the funds are deposited directly into your linked bank or brokerage settlement account. Ensure your broker has your correct bank details and your TIN, so withholding tax is applied correctly.

Corporate actions: Rights issues, bonus shares, and share splits all flow through your CSD account. If you are entitled to participate, your broker will notify you. Always respond before the deadline.

6. Tips for diaspora investors

Choose a broker that explicitly offers remote account opening and has experience serving diaspora clients.

Be aware that some documents may need to be notarised or apostilled in your country of residence. Ask your broker for a clear list of requirements upfront.

Factor in the cost and time of international fund transfers when planning your investments.

Put It Into Practice This week, contact two licensed brokers. Ask each for their fee schedule, their account opening requirements, and whether they offer an online trading platform. Compare their responses and choose one that best fits your needs.
