Case Study: CRDB Bank – A Fundamental Walk‑Through

By Amana Capital East Africa – Investor Education Series

This article applies the complete Amana Capital EA Stock Analysis Checklist (Article 4.7) to CRDB Bank Plc. Use it as a model for analysing any holding.

1. Macro Context

As of June 2026, Tanzania’s economy is growing at 5–6%, inflation is moderate, and the BOT policy rate is stable. This supports banking: loan demand is rising, credit costs manageable, and the exchange rate relatively stable. Favourable for well‑capitalised banks.

2. Industry Analysis

Banking is the largest DSE sector. It benefits from financial inclusion, mobile banking, and an expanding middle class. Competitors include NMB, KCB, and smaller domestic banks. CRDB has the largest branch network (a competitive moat), though NMB has a strong mobile and retail franchise.

3. Business Model & Moat

Traditional commercial banking: taking deposits and lending. Moats: wide branch network, decades‑old brand, high switching costs for corporate clients.

4. Financial Health (illustrative)

  • Revenue growth: low double‑digit, driven by loan book expansion and fee income.
  • NIM: ~6%, consistent with sector average.
  • ROE: ~16–18%.
  • NPLs: ~4.5%, within BOT comfort range.
  • CAR well above regulatory minimum.
  • Dividend: regular, moderate payout ratio; management retains earnings for growth.

5. Management Quality

Steady, conservative strategy. Clear communication in annual reports. No significant governance red flags. Diversified loan book.

6. Valuation (illustrative)

  • Share price: TZS 2,590
  • EPS: ~TZS 250 → P/E 10.4
  • Book value per share: ~TZS 1,450 → P/B 1.79
  • Dividend yield: ~3.5%

Compared to regional peer KCB (P/B ~1.0–1.2 on NSE), CRDB appears slightly more expensive on a book basis, but its higher ROE and growth potential may justify the premium. Historically, CRDB’s P/E has ranged 7–14, so 10.4 is in the middle — not a deep bargain, but not overvalued.

7. Technical Snapshot

Gradual uptrend since early 2026, support around 2,500, resistance near 2,650. Volume on up days healthy, suggesting accumulation.

8. Conclusion

CRDB presents a strong, moated bank with solid financials, capable management, and a reasonable valuation — but not a deep‑value opportunity. A long‑horizon investor might hold or accumulate on dips near 2,500, while a disciplined seller might trim if P/E exceeds 14 without a corresponding earnings increase.
