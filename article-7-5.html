Green Bonds and ESG Investing on the DSE

By Amana Capital East Africa – Investor Education Series

Global capital is shifting toward sustainable investing, and Tanzania is part of this movement. ESG stands for Environmental, Social, and Governance — factors that measure a company’s sustainability and ethical impact. On the DSE, this trend is visible through green bonds and growing governance expectations.

What Are Green Bonds?

Green bonds are debt instruments where the proceeds are exclusively used for environmentally beneficial projects — solar farms, reforestation, clean water, sustainable agriculture. The Tanzanian government and some corporations have begun issuing green and sustainable bonds. The DSE lists outstanding sustainable bonds with a face value of over TZS 531 billion (as of June 2026). The Government of Tanzania has also issued infrastructure bonds that may have green components.

Why ESG Matters for Investors

  • Risk management: Companies with poor governance (corruption, opaque accounting) or environmental liabilities (pollution) face fines, reputational damage, and higher costs.
  • Return potential: Sustainable companies may be better positioned for long‑term growth as regulations tighten and consumer preferences shift.
  • Access to capital: International investors increasingly demand ESG compliance. DSE companies that adopt strong ESG practices may attract more foreign capital, potentially boosting their share prices.

ESG on the DSE

While formal ESG ratings are not yet widespread in Tanzania, you can assess companies yourself:

  • Environmental: Does the company have a policy on emissions, waste, water usage? Does it face environmental litigation?
  • Social: How does it treat employees, communities, and customers? Does it promote financial inclusion?
  • Governance: Is the board independent? Are there conflicts of interest? Is executive pay reasonable and transparent?

Look for disclosures in annual reports and sustainability reports. Companies with strong governance — such as those with diverse, independent boards and clear audit trails — are generally lower risk.

Green Bond Investing

Green bonds trade on the DSE like conventional bonds. They offer fixed coupons and may provide an additional feel‑good factor, but you should still assess them based on yield, credit risk, and liquidity. The sustainable bond market in Tanzania is growing, and being aware of it positions you at the forefront of a global trend.
