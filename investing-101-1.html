When you buy a stock (or share), you are not simply buying a ticker symbol that wiggles on a screen. You are buying a fractional ownership stake in a real, operating business.

Ownership and Cash Flows

As a part-owner, you have a claim on the company's assets and its future cash flows. If the company grows its profits over time, the value of your ownership stake typically increases. Furthermore, many mature companies distribute a portion of their profits directly to shareholders in the form of dividends.

Why Invest in Stocks?

Historically, equities have been one of the most effective vehicles for preserving and growing purchasing power against inflation. While prices can be volatile in the short term, a long-horizon approach allows investors to benefit from the underlying economic growth of the region.
