How the DSE Works

The Dar es Salaam Stock Exchange (DSE) is a central marketplace where buyers and sellers trade shares of public companies. It provides liquidity and price discovery for Tanzania's capital markets.

The Ecosystem

  • The Exchange (DSE): The platform where trading occurs.
  • The Regulator (CMSA): The Capital Markets and Securities Authority ensures fair practice and protects investors.
  • Brokers: Licensed intermediaries who execute trades on behalf of clients. You cannot buy shares directly; you must use a broker.
  • The Depository (CSD): The Central Securities Depository holds electronic records of who owns which shares.

Trading and Settlement

When you buy a stock, the transaction follows a T+3 settlement cycle. This means the trade is officially settled and ownership transfers three business days after the transaction occurs.
